About

I graduated college from Brigham Young University with a bachelors degree in Exercise Science. During my time at BYU I also played on the rugby team winning 3 national championships and second team all american honors. My original plan was to go to Occupational Therapy school. After scoring well on the GRE I started applying to some schools around the country and even a university in Australia. In my first round of applications then I didn't get accepted to the schools that I applied for and was preparing to apply again. Shortly after my wife and I found out the she was pregnant. We decided that we wanted to have our own place to raise our son, instead of renting. We decided to stay here in Utah to be around family. At the time I was working with a company teaching people how to market online. I had also participated in a marketing intership. After graduating I decided I wanted to try a different job and that's when I started working at IDS.

IDS is a software company that built a web application for mortgage companies where they can input the information for a loan and generate data dynamic documents. After starting as a customer support agent then I became an account manager. As an account manager I manage 2 of the top 10 accounts in the company. For my clients I make customizations for them by writing custom code in documents, adjusting the code in calculation wizards (scripts), and coding custom loan programs. This made me want to get into web development/software engineering and become a web developer or software programmer. I initially started learning on my own but decided to apply to Lambda School to make sure that I was learning industry best practices and building a good coding base. I did a lot of research into which program I should join and in the end I chose Lambda Shcool because of their curriculum that consisted of computer science (many other programs did not cover any computer science). Now that I have finished Lambda I'm excited to start my new career in the software industry!

I currently live in Utah with my wife, Corina, and our two kids, Fox(2) and Grey(1).
